Appaloosa Horse Insurance

Appaloosa horse insurance reflects a breed with diverse use patterns — from competitive western performance and racing to trail riding, youth programs, and ranching — and a distinctive genetic health consideration that every Appaloosa owner should understand before purchasing insurance. The breed's long history in the American West, its recognition through the ApHC registry, and its active competition circuit all inform the insurance market. Appaloosas compete seriously at the ApHC World Show and in open western performance events, and high-level performance Appaloosas carry values that require formal underwriting attention.

Appaloosa Horse Club (ApHC) registration and the breed's distinctive spotting patterns do not directly affect standard insurance valuation — horses are insured based on market value, not coat color — but the registration pedigree and show credentials that accompany a well-bred Appaloosa do inform value assessments. Racing Appaloosas have a dedicated circuit through the American Indian Horse Registry and ApHC affiliates, and racing coverage for Appaloosas follows similar specialized requirements as thoroughbred racing coverage.

The most important Appaloosa-specific health consideration for insurance purposes is Equine Recurrent Uveitis (ERU), sometimes called moon blindness — an inflammatory eye condition that has a disproportionately high prevalence in Appaloosas relative to other breeds. ERU is a progressive condition that can lead to blindness, and horses known to have ERU may face exclusions or premium surcharges when underwriting is conducted. Pre-purchase ocular examinations by a veterinary ophthalmologist are strongly recommended for Appaloosas being purchased at significant values.

Key Point: Appaloosa buyers should include a veterinary ophthalmology examination in any pre-purchase evaluation for horses valued above $5,000. ERU identified at purchase should be disclosed to the insurer, and coverage exclusions for the affected eye(s) should be expected. Non-disclosed ERU discovered at claim time can result in claim denial.

Common Appaloosa Insurance Claims

  • Equine Recurrent Uveitis (ERU/moon blindness) — breed-elevated risk
  • Musculoskeletal injuries consistent with western performance use
  • Colic as leading cause of mortality
  • Night blindness (CSNB) in some Appaloosa lines
  • Joint conditions from competitive athletic use

Recommended Coverage for Appaloosa Horses

  • Agreed value mortality for ApHC-registered performance horses
  • Major medical with ophthalmic exclusion documentation if ERU present
  • Racing coverage for Appaloosa circuit competitors
  • Youth and 4-H program horse coverage
  • Trail and pleasure use coverage for recreational horses
