Horse Insurance Claims — How the Process Works

Filing a horse insurance claim correctly and promptly is essential to receiving timely payment and avoiding disputes. The claims process for equine insurance follows a defined sequence that horse owners should understand before they ever need to use it — ideally reviewing the claims requirements at the time of policy purchase, not in the emergency moment when a horse is in distress.

The most critical first step in any potential claim is notifying your insurer as soon as possible. Most equine mortality policies require that the insurer be notified before euthanasia is authorized — except in cases of extreme emergency where a veterinarian certifies that immediate euthanasia is required to prevent suffering. Failure to notify before euthanasia is one of the most common causes of claim denial, and it occurs most often when an owner in an emotional crisis focuses on the horse's suffering rather than the insurance requirement. Put your insurer's emergency claim number in your phone contacts now.

After initial notification, the insurer typically requires a veterinary certificate of the cause of death or the medical event that is the basis of the claim. This certificate must be completed by a licensed veterinarian and must identify the specific cause of death or injury, the treatment provided, and — in the case of euthanasia — a statement that euthanasia was performed humanely and was medically necessary. The veterinary certificate is the foundational document for a mortality claim, and its completeness directly affects claim processing speed.

Documentation requirements for a complete claim typically include the veterinary certificate, proof of ownership, policy documentation, original purchase documents if the claim involves a dispute over insured value, and any prior veterinary records that the insurer requests to evaluate the pre-existing condition question. Major medical claims additionally require itemized invoices from treating facilities, documentation of the diagnosis, and evidence that the condition was not pre-existing or excluded. Processing timelines for straightforward claims are typically 30 to 60 days after complete documentation is received.

Key Point: Keep a file with all of your horse's veterinary records, vaccination history, and insurance documentation in one accessible location — and know where it is. When a crisis occurs, having this documentation immediately at hand dramatically streamlines the claims process.
