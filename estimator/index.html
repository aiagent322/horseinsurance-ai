How Equine Insurance Premiums Are Calculated

📊

Mortality Rate

Most insurers price mortality at 2.5%–4.5% of insured value annually. Performance horses typically land at 3%–3.5%. The rate reflects statistical loss ratios across thousands of horses.

🏇

Discipline Risk

High-stress disciplines like cutting and reining carry higher risk due to intense athletic demand. Rope horses face trailer and arena hazards. Pleasure horses are typically the lowest risk category.

🩺

Major Medical

Colic surgery alone can run $8,000–$15,000. Major medical coverage adds a flat per-horse premium regardless of value, making it one of the highest-value add-ons for working horses.

📋

Underwriting Factors

Vet exam results, prior claims, age, and training history all affect your actual quote. Horses over 15 or under 3 typically face loading charges. Clean health histories can earn discounts.
